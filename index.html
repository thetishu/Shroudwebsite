History at a Glance

  1. c. 1354 — First well-documented exhibition in Lirey, France.
  2. 1532 — Fire at Chambéry; documented scorch marks and repairs.
  3. 1578 — Moved to Turin under House of Savoy custodianship.
  4. 1898 — Secondo Pia’s photographs reveal negative-like detail.
  5. 1978 — STURP interdisciplinary study.
  6. 1988 — Radiocarbon dating reported medieval age (subject to debate).
  7. 2002 — Major conservation and restoration program.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is the image considered unusual?

The image is extremely superficial (on the topmost fibrils), shows negative-like contrast in early photography, and contains 3D-like depth information — characteristics not easily explained by known medieval painting techniques.

What tests have been run on the Shroud?

Imaging (UV/IR), spectroscopy, microscopy, pollen analysis, bloodstain testing, and radiocarbon dating — results have often been debated and reinterpreted.

Is the Shroud on display today?

The Shroud is housed in Turin's cathedral. Public exhibitions are occasional and announced by the custodial authorities.
